Ayurvedic Home Remedies

Simple, time-tested ideas you can try at home using common kitchen ingredients. Perfect for mild, non-emergency discomforts—always seek medical care for serious or persistent symptoms.

Cold

Runny nose · Sneezing · Mild congestion

  • Bring 1 cup water to a simmer with 4 tulsi leaves, 1 tsp grated ginger, and 3 crushed peppercorns.
  • Let it steep 5 minutes, strain, add 1 tsp honey + a squeeze of lemon.
  • Sip twice daily to clear nasal passages and support immunity.

Skip honey for children under 1 year.

Fever

Body warmth · Tiredness · Mild aches

  • Mix 1 tsp coriander seeds, 1/2 tsp cumin, and 1/2 tsp fennel; boil in 2 cups water for 10 minutes.
  • Add a pinch of cardamom powder and let it cool to lukewarm.
  • Drink small sips through the day to stay hydrated and support digestion.

Seek medical care if temperature > 101°F (38.3°C) or persists over 48 hours.

Cough

Tickle in throat · Dry cough spells

  • Combine 1 tsp raw honey with 1/4 tsp fresh ginger juice and a pinch of black pepper.
  • Take slowly after meals, up to 3 times daily.
  • Follow with warm water to keep the throat moist.

Honey is not suitable for infants. Consult a doctor for chronic cough.

Sore Throat

Scratchy throat · Mild pain while swallowing

  • Stir 1/2 tsp rock salt and 1/4 tsp turmeric into a cup of warm water.
  • Gargle for 30 seconds, repeat 3–4 times daily.
  • Follow with a teaspoon of clarified butter (ghee) if the throat feels very dry.

Do not swallow large amounts of the gargle solution.

Minor Cuts

Small nicks · Superficial scrapes

  • Clean the area gently with mild soap and water; pat dry.
  • Make a paste of turmeric + coconut oil; apply a thin layer as an antimicrobial shield.
  • Cover with breathable gauze and replace twice daily.

Seek urgent care for deep, bleeding, or infected wounds.

Pimples

Small breakouts · Occasional redness

  • Blend equal parts multani mitti (Fuller&apos;s earth) and sandalwood powder with rose water.
  • Apply as a thin mask over affected areas for 10 minutes.
  • Rinse with cool water and dab on fresh aloe gel to calm the skin.

Patch test first if you have sensitive skin.

Indigestion

Bloating · Mild nausea · Heaviness after meals

  • Dry roast equal parts cumin, fennel, and ajwain (carom). Grind into a coarse powder.
  • After meals, mix 1/2 tsp of the blend with warm water and a squeeze of lime.
  • Chew a small piece of roasted ginger for additional relief.

Avoid if you have ulcers or are on antacid medication without medical advice.

Quick Safety Reminders

  • Always wash ingredients thoroughly and use clean utensils.
  • Avoid these remedies if you are allergic to any listed component.
  • Pregnant, lactating individuals, or people on prescription medicines should consult a doctor first.
  • Discontinue immediately if symptoms worsen or new reactions appear.

Helpful Extras

  • Stay hydrated with warm water infused with cumin or coriander.
  • Prioritize rest and light, freshly cooked meals when unwell.
  • Document what works for you to build a personalized wellness diary.
